Two Ways I Help

Your Team's AI Velocity Dropped

The Problem: You adopted AI dev tools like Cursor, Claude, or Copilot. PRs got longer. Bugs multiplied. Code reviews became archaeology. Your devs are copy-pasting AI-generated code and calling it done. Velocity tanked.

What I Do:

  • Audit your current AI tool usage and identify where slop enters the codebase
  • Establish AI-assisted development standards and quality gates
  • Train teams on effective prompting, testing, and review practices for AI-generated code
  • Implement governance: when to use AI, when not to, and how to catch bad outputs
  • Set up metrics to actually measure velocity improvement vs. code churn

Result: Teams ship faster with higher quality. AI becomes a force multiplier, not a mess generator.

Your Team Has Shadow AI—And It's Costing You

The Problem: You haven't officially adopted AI tools, but your engineers are using them anyway—personal Claude accounts, Cursor subscriptions, ChatGPT Plus. It's costing $150+ per engineer per month. Legal wants policies. IT wants approvals. Meanwhile, velocity is down because nobody's coordinating. You need to formalize AI adoption before the chaos gets worse.

What I Do:

  • Assess the actual AI tools your team is using (officially or unofficially)
  • Design a coordinated AI tool strategy: what to standardize, what to allow, what to ban
  • Establish practical governance and policies that engineers will actually follow
  • Negotiate enterprise licenses and consolidate shadow subscriptions to reduce costs
  • Create shared practices so teams use AI as a coordinated unit, not isolated individuals

Result: Lower costs, higher velocity, and a team that actually works together instead of 50 individuals doing their own thing.
